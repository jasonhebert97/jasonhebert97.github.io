BROWN UNIVERSITY OFFICE OF SUSTAINABILITY MARKETING PLAN

As Brown University's Office of Sustainability (OS from here on) transitioned to using the general University's visual theming policies and standards, it was important to effectively articulate the Office's new marketing tactics and streamline the entire flow for the interns.

All materials presented below were made and organized by me.

The Marketing Website for Interns

This preview of the website -- not accessible to the public -- depicts the interface through which the current interns for the Office of Sustainability can submit a variety of requests pertaining to different marketing practices.

Social Media & Today@Brown Request Forms

On this website, OS interns can submit both social media and Today@Brown (a proprietary newsletter sent to each community member daily) requests.

For submitting social media requests, the interns have two options: one being a time-sensitive form (for those posts requiring more specific dates and times for posting) and a filler form (for those posts that are meant to fill in any empty space between time-sensitive posts).

Today@Brown submissions are handled the exact same way as social media submissions.

Each of the forms were made using Google Forms and automatically collect the data. Examples of the forms are shown below:

The time-sensitive social media form.

The filler social media form.

All of the responses submitted by the interns are processed automatically by the linked Google Sheet and are presented on an automated calendar for the marketing intern to parse out quickly and easily. All the information is computed via Google Sheet functions and formulas, requiring no extra labor. An example of a calendar month with automatically populated posts is shown below:

Example of the social media calendar.

Graphics Request Form

Similar to the social media and Today@Brown request forms described above, fellow interns are able to submit Graphic Requests using a similar Google Form. A Google Sheet which is automated the same way pre-populates the set calendar sheets in order for the design intern to quickly parse out first-draft due dates.

Data Visualization Templates

An important addition to the marketing plan was creating a streamlined means of producting data visualizations that were cognizant of the newly accepted university visual standards. Four different types of graphs were made into templates: vertical bar, horizontal bar, line, and pie. All templates were made using Microsoft Excel due to its flexibility regarding graphics.
