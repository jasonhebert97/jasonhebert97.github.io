About me...

I am a fourth-year student at Brown University studying Modern Culture and Media with an emphasis on the technical and sociocultural influences and developments of modern computational technologies. I prefer the pronouns 'they', 'them', and 'theirs' or 'he', 'him', and 'his'; am racially diverse (Filipino, Abenaki Native American, French, and English); and am a first-generation college student. I am familiar with many Adobe Applications (PhotoShop, InDesign, Illustrator, and Animate), and have a background in marketing, design, and communications.

Particularly, I hope to understand modern media technologies through a humanitarian and sociocultural lens, while nonetheless developing the skills needed to effectively create within the field of computer-mediated production. I intend on taking courses within Brown's computer science department as well as digital media courses at RISD and classes in the arts and humanities. Coming from a diverse background, diversity, equity, and positionality are crucial to my identity, so I work to complicate the White, cisheteronormative, and masculine-dominated computer science field by understanding computation through racialized, gendered, and queer perspectives.
